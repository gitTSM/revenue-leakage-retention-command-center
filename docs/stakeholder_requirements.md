# Stakeholder Requirements & Use Cases

## Overview
This document outlines the key stakeholder groups, their objectives, and the primary business use cases that the Revenue Leakage & Retention Command Center is designed to support.

The goal is to ensure alignment between business needs and analytical outputs, enabling actionable, data-driven decision-making across the organization.

---

## Stakeholder Groups & Objectives

### 1. Executive Leadership (CEO / COO)

**Primary Objective:**
Understand overall revenue health and identify areas requiring strategic intervention.

**Key Concerns:**
- Is revenue growing, stable, or declining?
- How much revenue is being lost, and why?
- Are current strategies effective in retaining customers?

---

### 2. VP of Revenue / Sales Leadership

**Primary Objective:**
Maximize revenue retention and growth across existing customer base.

**Key Concerns:**
- Which customers are at risk of churn or downgrade?
- What are the main drivers of revenue loss?
- Where should retention efforts be focused?

---

### 3. Finance Team

**Primary Objective:**
Ensure accuracy and completeness of revenue realization.

**Key Concerns:**
- How much contracted revenue is not being realized?
- Are there delays or inefficiencies in collections?
- What is the impact of discounting and write-offs?

---

### 4. Customer Success Team

**Primary Objective:**
Proactively prevent churn and improve customer health.

**Key Concerns:**
- Which customers require immediate intervention?
- What behavioral signals indicate churn risk?
- How do usage and support patterns affect retention?

---

### 5. Sales / Account Managers

**Primary Objective:**
Manage customer portfolios effectively and meet revenue targets.

**Key Concerns:**
- Which accounts are underperforming?
- How is my portfolio trending over time?
- Where should I prioritize outreach?

---

## Core Business Use Cases

---

### Use Case 1: Executive Revenue Monitoring

**Business Question:**
What is the current state of revenue performance and how is it trending over time?

**Why It Matters:**
Leadership requires a clear and immediate understanding of revenue health to make strategic decisions and allocate resources effectively.

**Required Data:**
- total contracted revenue  
- realized revenue  
- revenue leakage  
- renewal rates  
- churn and downgrade metrics  

**Expected Output:**
- KPI summary (revenue, leakage, retention)
- trend analysis over time
- high-level breakdown by region, segment, and product  

---

### Use Case 2: Revenue Leakage Diagnosis

**Business Question:**
Where is revenue being lost and what are the primary drivers?

**Why It Matters:**
Identifying leakage sources enables targeted interventions and improves revenue realization.

**Required Data:**
- contracts  
- revenue records  
- discounts  
- renewals  
- payment and write-off data  

**Expected Output:**
- leakage breakdown by driver (churn, downgrade, discount, late payment)
- leakage by region, product, and segment  
- comparison of contracted vs realized revenue  

---

### Use Case 3: Customer Risk Identification

**Business Question:**
Which customers are at risk of churn or revenue loss?

**Why It Matters:**
Early identification allows proactive engagement and reduces preventable churn.

**Required Data:**
- usage metrics  
- support tickets  
- payment behavior  
- renewal timelines  

**Expected Output:**
- risk scoring model  
- list of high-risk customers  
- segmentation by risk level  
- key risk indicators per customer  

---

### Use Case 4: Retention & Renewal Management

**Business Question:**
How effectively are we renewing existing customers?

**Why It Matters:**
Renewals are a critical driver of recurring revenue and long-term growth.

**Required Data:**
- renewal schedules  
- renewal outcomes  
- contract values  
- downgrade and churn data  

**Expected Output:**
- renewal rate trends  
- upcoming renewals pipeline  
- renewal performance by segment and account manager  

---

### Use Case 5: Account Manager Performance

**Business Question:**
How does performance vary across account managers and regions?

**Why It Matters:**
Understanding performance differences helps improve accountability and optimize resource allocation.

**Required Data:**
- account ownership  
- revenue performance  
- churn and renewal outcomes  
- discounting behavior  

**Expected Output:**
- performance comparison across managers  
- leakage and churn by portfolio  
- identification of outliers  

---

### Use Case 6: Customer-Level Drilldown

**Business Question:**
What is happening with a specific customer account?

**Why It Matters:**
Enables operational teams to take targeted actions at the individual account level.

**Required Data:**
- full customer history (revenue, usage, tickets, payments, renewals)  

**Expected Output:**
- detailed customer profile  
- timeline of activity and events  
- explanation of risk drivers
